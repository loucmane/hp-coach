# gen-las-short-2 — det kontantfria samhället (debatt)

## Genre / topic rationale
LÄS short, sakprosa / debatt_opinion. Topic: the cashless society — a
first-person op-ed arguing that the drift to cashless has outrun the groups it
strands, and that the issue is resilience and inclusion (a guaranteed fallback),
not nostalgia for coins. Opening move = framing-claim ("framsteg för vem?");
normative modality (*bör/måste*) and an explicit stance throughout, per the
debatt preset. Topic is outside the batch 1–7 used list (nearest neighbour would
be crisis preparedness, which was batch-used only as civil-defence stockpiling;
this is specifically payment infrastructure and financial inclusion) and is my
exclusive pool item. Signed by a byline; no closing question.

Law 9: the passage grants the cost objection ("Det stämmer"), concedes the
digital system's convenience (the author pays by phone himself), and carries
concrete residue (the merchant quote, the winter week with the network down)
that does not all serve the thesis.

## Planted trap architecture
Planted hedged/directional/scoped target (detail): when the last bank office
closes, cash demand does **not** vanish — it **concentrates** on a few holdout
merchants who bear a growing burden (long deposit trips, robbery risk, duty over
profit). Second target: the author's explicit stance — the right to pay cash
must not be left to the market alone.

- **Q1 (detalj_ospecificerad, anchored on Holmström), key B.** Paraphrase of the
  concentration finding. A = reversed_causality (falling demand -> banks
  withdraw, arrow flipped, hidden under "tycktes"); C = overgeneralisation that
  contradicts the text ("upphörde helt" vs "efterfrågan hänger kvar"); D =
  scope_shift to the crisis/blackout paragraph (the author's resilience
  argument, not Holmström's finding).
- **Q2 (forfattarens_hallning), key A.** The author's explicit thesis. B =
  reversed stance / plausible_worldknowledge (the "cash is better" view he
  expressly denies); C = detail_as_main (the cost concession he grants then
  argues against); D = overgeneralisation past his stated refusal to "vrida
  klockan tillbaka" (cash as *primary* means).

## Hedge balance (Law 10)
Q1 key B is the qualified/measured claim, but distractor A is also hedged
("tycktes"), so the key is not the sole measured option among absolutised
distractors. Q2 key A is the confident normative claim sitting among absolutised
distractors (B "tryggare och bättre", D "huvudsakliga ... hela samhället"). So
"correct = qualified" holds on neither question uniformly — the correlation is
broken across the unit. Key is never the longest option (Q1 longest = A; Q2
longest = C). Keys spread B/A.

## Self-blind-solve
Solved both from the passage alone, arguing for each non-keyed option:
- Q1 → B only. A reverses the causal arrow; C says the opposite of "efterfrågan
  hänger kvar"; D belongs to the crisis paragraph, not the survey finding.
- Q2 → A only. B is the stance he denies; C is a dismissed concession; D
  over-shoots his explicit "inte för att vrida klockan tillbaka".
