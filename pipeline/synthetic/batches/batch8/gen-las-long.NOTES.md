# gen-las-long — authoring notes

## Topic & genre
- **Topic (exclusive to this unit):** Svenska humleodlingens fall och återkomst — the decline
  and modern return of Swedish hop farming, told through a fictional archival study.
- **Genre:** sakprosa / facktext_larobok, result-lede opening. A populärvetenskaplig
  agrarhistoria voice — an ethnologist reads 18th–19th-century parish tithe and
  husförhör records and finds a hedged, place-and-demand-conditioned survival pattern.
- **Distance from used topics:** no overlap with batches 1–7. Nearest neighbours are
  seed banks / fäbodbruk / repair-economy (all agrarian-adjacent), but this is a distinct
  crop-history + landscape-geology + local-market thesis, not seed preservation, alpine
  pasturing, or reuse. No pollinator/bee content (batch material) despite the botanical setting.

## Real-vs-invented boundary (Law 1 / M-PLAGIARISM)
- **Background flavour only (never the tested claim):** the medieval duty to keep a
  humlegård and the 1800s collapse under cheap continental imports are genuine

  the 1870s" was — atmosphere, not the measured finding.
- **Every measured result is fictional and passage-internal:** Märta Hägglund, Vreta

## Planted trap architecture (passage & questions are one design)
The passage plants three hedged/directional/scoped hooks:
1. **Finding (P1/P4):** brewer-demand → garden-survival, *scoped* to well-drained esker soil
   ("men bara där … torr, väldränerad mark"; "oavsett hur nära bryggaren låg" on clay).
2. **Mechanism (P4):** hops are perishable → distant transport destroys value → no nearby
   buyer means no reason to keep the garden. Roots "lätt tog skada av stående väta."
3. **Caveats (P6/P7):** a dozen parishes show nothing; beer-tradition confound;
   Rehn's bookkeeping-bias objection; "ett mönster, inte en lag."

  faithfully-true detail.

## Self-blind-solve (skeptical, passage alone)
Solved all four arguing actively for each non-keyed option:
- **Q1 = B.** A inverts the causal arrow the text states; C is killed by the explicit soil
  scope ("oavsett hur nära bryggaren låg" the clay gardens died first); D names the method,
  not the finding. Single defensible answer.
- **Q2 = C.** A/B/D each contradict P2 (own garden per farm; poles not tithe-delivery; import
  post-dates and *ends* the duty). C is the only faithful paraphrase.
- **Q3 = A.** Requires combining "roots damaged by standing wet" + "esker soil drains" in the
  text's own direction. B flips it to brewer-siting (text: soil mattered independent of the
  brewer); C substitutes a warmth mechanism the text never gives; D over-absolutises.
- **Q4 = D.** Spans lede + finding + caveats + the return; A/B/C each fail as whole-text
  summaries with a nameable flaw. Single defensible answer.

No question is two-way. All entities fictional; no famous-thesis anchoring.
