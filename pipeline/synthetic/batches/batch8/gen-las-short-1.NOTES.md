# gen-las-short-1 — fjällledernas underhåll och slitage (essä)

## Genre / topic rationale
LÄS short, sakprosa / essä (reflective, first-person-light with a named
fictional fieldworker's data). Topic: mountain-trail (fjälled) wear and
maintenance — an essay arguing, from a fictional survey, that trail erosion is
governed less by visitor numbers than by where the path crosses **wet ground**,
because walkers braid the trail wider to avoid puddles. Opening move =
framing-claim ("the answer is rarely what you'd guess"); the passage closes on a
reflection about invisible maintenance and is signed by a byline (0 % of
authentic passages close on a question). Topic is trail maintenance / wear, NOT
pastoral farming (fäbodbruk was batch-used and is explicitly excluded); it is my
exclusive pool item and does not neighbour any batch 1–7 topic.

Law 9 (no manufactured tidiness): the passage grants the steep-slope exception,
states the pattern is "inte entydig", and carries concrete residue (the two
seasons, ~80 sections, the gyttjepöl, källdrag) that does not all point at the
thesis.

## Planted trap architecture
Planted hedged/directional/scoped target: worst wear was **almost always where
the trail crossed wet ground**, and dry stony ground kept few tracks even under
heavy traffic — driven by avoidance (väjande), not pressure. Second target: the
whole-text thesis that small interventions (spänger) beat visitor bans, though
not uniformly.

- **Q1 (enligt_texten_detalj), key A.** Paraphrase of the wet-ground finding.
  B = overgeneralisation that is also the *explicitly refuted* traffic
  hypothesis ("genomgående ... oavsett"); C = reversed_causality in hedged form
  (flips which ground type widened, keeping "tycktes/oftast" so the key is not
  the sole hedged option); D = scope_shift to the steep-slope caveat, which the
  text never names as the site of greatest wear.
- **Q2 (huvudbudskap_syfte, a "bäst"-item), key C.** Whole-text claim, kept
  qualified via "beror mer ... än". Law 11 honoured — no verbatim-true
  distractor: A distorts the method (they measured **width**, not slope) so it
  carries an identifiable flaw on top of being a detail; B overgeneralises
  "biter föga" into "alla ... verkningslösa"; D lifts the local slope caveat and
  adds an unstated comparison ("mer än vätan gör").

## Hedge balance (Law 10)
Q1 key A reads as a firm contrast ("där ... blöt mark, inte där flest gick")
while distractor C over-hedges and is wrong — so "pick the qualified option"
fails on Q1. Q2 key C is the qualified one. The correlation between "correct"
and "hedged" is therefore broken across the unit. Key is never the longest
option (Q1 longest = D; Q2 longest = B). Keys spread A/C.

## Self-blind-solve
Solved both from the passage alone, arguing for each non-keyed option:
- Q1 → A only. B is the refuted traffic hypothesis absolutised; C reverses the
  wet/dry roles; D promotes a caveat the text never designates as greatest wear.
- Q2 → C only. A is a distorted method delspår; B over-shoots the "biter föga"
  hedge; D is a local caveat with an added, unstated comparison.
