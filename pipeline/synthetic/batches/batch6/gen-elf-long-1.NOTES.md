## The world-knowledge-defeating architecture (the core design)
The naive story any solver already "knows" — *more seed varieties banked = more crop diversity insured; a fuller vault is a safer one* — is deliberately made **true on the axis everyone watches** and **reversed on the hidden axis**, exactly the shape that killed passage-blind solving in the batch-5 cullet unit:

- **Intake / coverage axis ("just as advertised"):** each consignment of accessions brings in genuinely new varieties, *across the whole tracked range*, in both the capacity-matched and the outrunning sections alike. You cannot fault "collecting adds diversity" — it is real on both arms.
- **Viability axis (the hidden reversal):** seeds decay in storage and must be **regenerated** (grown out to a fresh crop) before they die; a store has finite regeneration throughput, so the bigger the collection, the longer each accession waits its turn. In capacity-matched sections the living share barely stirs even at large size; in outrunning sections it edges down slowly then **falls sharply once the collection passes ~4× a season's regeneration capacity**, and a dead accession must be re-collected from the field (if it still exists) — handing back the diversity the banking secured.

Net verdict (¶5): the number that matters is not how many accessions a vault holds, but how many it can keep alive — **regeneration capacity, not shelf count, sets the real ceiling.** A solver armed only with "seed banking is good" gets Q3/Q4/Q5 wrong; you must have read *this* study's viability finding and its bounded verdict.

## Arc & devices
phenomenon (fuller vault pitched as straightforwardly safer) → evidence (matched decade of intake; each consignment adds new varieties "right across the range they tracked") → complication ("Then Sahlberg's team stopped counting jars and started opening them" — the pivot; living share falls past ~4× capacity) → reinterpretation (Renner's absolute quote vs Sahlberg's "more guarded" capacity-costs-money view) → verdict (¶5, bounded: "not how many accessions a vault holds. It is how many it can keep alive."). One **load-bearing quotation** from Renner, deliberately over-absolute ("always, whatever the shelf already holds … no such thing as too many accessions") to seed the attribution-swap trap. Sentence rhythm alternates 40–50-word subordinated sentences with short verdicts ("Measured by distinct varieties taken in, more collecting was simply more diversity."; "Count the jars, and the safest vault is the fullest. Open them, and it is not."; "It is how many it can keep alive."). Seeded hedges as trap anchors: "right across the range they tracked", "barely stirred", "about four times what one season could regenerate", "only so far", "where regeneration keeps pace".

Anti-tidiness (law 9): the passage argues *against* its own opening pitch, carries concrete digression (the physical mechanics of decay and re-collection, the "plots and hands being finite"), and leaves residue (the dead jars, the field hunt) that does not all point at the key.

## STANCE-ITEM design (Q5) — hedge-balanced two-hedge shape
Following the batch-5-proven fix for the G-STEM STRUCTURAL_LEAK kill ("the only hedged/qualified option among absolutes"). Q5 options **A and B are both cautious/qualified in form** — A "Broadly persuaded: she largely accepts…", B (key) "Qualified: she grants… but holds…". C and D are the absolutes (attribution-swap "Convinced", polarity-overshoot "Dismissive"). Because two options are hedged and two are absolute, **no hedge-form tell survives** — the item is decided only by *direction/content*: does the writer treat the seed deaths as a **minor fixable gap** (A, siding with Renner) or as the **binding ceiling** (B, the passage's actual verdict)? A is the **hedged-in-form-but-false-in-content distractor** the brief prescribes (a direction reversal), defeated by ¶5's "no store she knows of can regenerate its whole holding fast enough to bank without limit" and the collection that outruns regeneration "has a ceiling".

## DISTRACTOR PLAUSIBILITY audit (the current #1 kill cause)
Every distractor is a **plausible-but-wrong mechanism** a passage-blind reader cannot dismiss, and each trap label names the ACTUAL mechanism — no absurd overclaims, no circular consequence-as-cause, no off-topic options in the main-idea item:
- **Q1** A/C/D are all genuinely on-topic and internally sensible (the pitch, a real conservation-policy stance, the storage-decay fact) — the flaw in each is scope/attribution, not absurdity.
- **Q2** B keeps the passage's real fact and only over-extends its scope (a smart reader who half-remembers "collecting adds varieties" finds it live); C mis-assigns the passage's central split; D invents a plausible provenance.
- **Q3** A is verbatim-plausible (it is the *other* arm's real curve); B is the intuitive "it went bad, so it went bad at once"; D is a sensible-sounding operational fix.
- **Q4** A ("hardier species") is the single most natural real-world explanation and is defeated only by the passage's held-steady species; B over-absolutizes against Sahlberg; C is a true rule that simply doesn't discriminate the two arms.
- **Q5** all three distractors are live stances; A in particular is engineered to be as hedged as the key.

- **Key is the single longest option in ZERO of 5 questions** (longest per q is a distractor: Q1→D, Q2→B, Q3→D, Q4→C, Q5→A). Stronger than batch 5 (which had the key single-longest on 1 of 5). M-TELL passes clean.
