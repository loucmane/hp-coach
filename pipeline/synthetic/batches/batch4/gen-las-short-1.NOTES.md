# gen-las-short-1 — authoring notes

## Genre / topic rationale
- **Topic:** urban ecology in Sweden — hedgehog (igelkott) nocturnal movement across allotment-garden (koloniträdgård) fences and the connectivity of city green space. Sits squarely in this batch's Unit-1 pool (city wildlife + allotment gardens + park management) and deliberately avoids pollinators. No neighbouring on any batch 1–3 topic (nothing about pollinators/meadows, gentrification, living walls, etc.).
- **Genre:** sakprosa / populärvetenskap, result-lede opening (`Det blev tvärtom`). Nominalisation + `-s`-passives present, one embedded expert quote, byline close on a fictional science journalist. No glossary — no specialist term needs one, so none was invented (avoids the Batch-1 glossary-defines-absent-word kill).
- **Anti-tidiness:** the finding is deliberately non-monotone (tidy lots with gaps show nothing if neighbours are walled; leaf piles alone do nothing; gaps also admit foxes/badgers and can funnel animals into traffic). The passage carries residue that does not all point at the answer.

## Planted trap architecture
**Q1 — enligt_texten_detalj (key B).** Planted target (p1): movement `tycktes ... bli både längre och tätare — men bara där flera lotter i rad släppte igenom djuren` (hedged / directional / scoped to connected lots).
- A = **reversed_causality** — arrow flipped (movement → gardeners cutting gaps).
- C = **overgeneralisation** — `oavsett hur grannlotterna såg ut` strips the connectivity condition; contradicted by the p3 caveat.
- D = **detail_as_main** — the camera method (`drygt niotusen passager`) dressed as the result.

**Q2 — forfattarens_hallning (key C).** Stance target: `Det avgörande är att områdena hänger ihop` + `Att bara låta lotten växa igen är därför ingen genväg`.
- A = **stance_inversion** (NOT reversed_causality) — the "let it grow wild / leaf piles are the decisive action" view she explicitly rejects.
- B = **overgeneralisation** — `säkraste sättet` pushed past her `Nyttan måste ändå vägas mot annat` caveat.
- D = **plausible_worldknowledge** — traffic-as-main-cause of decline; text uses traffic only as a *risk of connectivity*, never as the cause of the hedgehog's status.

## Stem-law compliance
Q1 stem ("Vad fann studien om igelkottarnas rörelser…") names the setup but the outcome could have gone either way (higher / lower / no difference). Q2 stem asks for a stance the passage could have argued in several directions. Neither stem entails its key.

## Distractor-form-law compliance
No distractor is eliminable by bare form. In Q2 two options (C key, D) are non-absolute, so "pick the hedged one" fails; A and B carry absolutes but each is refutable only by passage *content*. The key is the longest option in Q1 only (in Q2 distractor A is longest), breaking the length/qualified tell across the unit.

## Self-blind-solve
Solved both from the passage alone, arguing for every non-keyed option. Q1 = B uniquely (scope condition kills C, arrow kills A, method kills D). Q2 = C uniquely (A is the rejected stance, B over-absolutises, D is an unstated cause). Key letters spread B, C. All entities fictional. Passes M-SCHEMA / M-BANDS / M-TELL / M-PLAGIARISM.
