# gen-las-short-2 — authoring notes

**Unit:** LÄS short, POPULÄRVETENSKAP (sakprosa). `family: kontorsakustik-popularvetenskap-short`.
**Title:** *När ett jämnt brus dämpar det öppna kontoret* · byline *Harriet Lindqvist,
vetenskapsredaktör* · glossary: `maskeringsljud = jämnt bakgrundsljud som ska dölja störande ljud`
(the term appears in p3).
**Opening move:** result-lede (study found an unexpected result), nominal register with `-s`-passive
material.

## Topic rationale
Workplace acoustics / cognition: a fictional sound-masking trial in open-plan offices. Distinct
domain from unit-1 (transport policy) and from every forbidden topic. Entities all fictional:
*Nadia Selander*, *Institutionen för arbetsmiljöpsykologi i Härnösand*, *Harriet Lindqvist*, the
2022–2023 pink-noise trial. No famous thesis — the concrete residue (task-split finding, ~300
diarists, twelve offices, age spread) cannot be pre-answered from general knowledge. The passage
deliberately carries digression and unresolved residue (a couple of offices saw no effect; younger
staff bothered less "med stor spridning") to avoid the frictionless-parable AI tell (law 9).

## Planted trap architecture

**Q1 — `enligt_texten_detalj`** (key **B**). Planted target (p3): masking *"tycktes … hjälpa"* at
routine tasks but *"gick skattningarna åt andra hållet"* at demanding ones. Hedged (*tycktes*),
directional (masking → concentration), scoped (routine only).
- **A = overgeneralisation** — *"alla slags arbetsuppgifter, oavsett hur krävande"* absolutises the
  conditional finding the text calls *"villkorad, inte universell"*.
- **C = reversed_causality** — genuine arrow flip: already-focused staff *chose* the brus, vs the
  text's masking → concentration; also contradicts that researchers, not employees, switched it on.
- **D = scope_shift** — promotes the diary method (p2) to "the study's finding".

**Q2 — `huvudbudskap_syfte`** (key **C**). Whole-text claim spanning lede + finding + caveat:
small acoustic interventions help but the effect is conditional and task-dependent.
- **A = detail_as_main** — a true p2 sub-point (a nearby conversation distracts more than steady
  traffic) offered as the main message.
- **B = overgeneralisation** — "the reliable solution to most problems", pushing past the text's
  explicit warning against selling brus as *"en billig lösning på allt som skaver"*.
- **D = scope_shift / true_but_irrelevant** — the diary method again, as the message.

## Hedge/length-tell breaking
Q1 key is hedged, but its reversed_causality distractor (C) also carries a soft quantifier
(*oftare än andra*), so "qualified" doesn't single out the key. In Q2, distractor A is an equally
measured, non-absolute claim; only B is absolute. Combined with unit-1's Q2 (confident = correct),
the batch does not let "hedged = key" score blind. Key letters spread **B, C**. M-TELL confirms the
key is the longest option in **0/2** questions (Q1 longest=C, Q2 longest=A).

## Self-blind-solve (skeptical, passage-only)
- **Q1 → B.** A absolutises the explicitly conditional finding; C flips the causal arrow and
  contradicts who controlled the brus; D is the method, not the result. Single defensible answer.
- **Q2 → C.** A and D are true sub-points that fail as whole-text summaries; B overgeneralises past
  the stated caveat. Single defensible answer.
Neither item is two-way.
