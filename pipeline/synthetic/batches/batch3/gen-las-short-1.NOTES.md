# gen-las-short-1 — authoring notes

**Unit:** LÄS short, DEBATT / opinion (sakprosa). `family: nattagssubvention-debatt-short`.
**Title:** *Nattåget hör hemma i infrastrukturbudgeten* · byline *Elias Bohlin, transportforskare och debattör* · no glossary (no specialist term needing one).
**Opening move:** framing-claim. First-person + normative modality (*måste*, *bör*), stance explicit — the debatt preset.

## Topic rationale
Transport policy: whether sleeper-train subsidies should be judged as infrastructure or as a
loss item. Deliberately distinct from every forbidden topic (nocturnal pollinators, urban-quarter
renewal, board-game cafés, fog-nets, four-day week, school-lunch kitchens, church organs, mural
conservation, self-healing concrete) and from unit-2's domain (workplace acoustics). All entities
fictional: *Elias Bohlin*, *Trafikanalytiska rådet*, the Stockholm–Narvik winter price trial.
No famous thesis is leaned on — a knowledgeable reader cannot pre-answer without the passage's
invented numbers (quarter price cut, weekday-only, longest-legs-only).

## Planted trap architecture
The passage owns one hedged+directional+scoped claim per question.

**Q1 — `enligt_texten_detalj`** (key **C**). Planted target (p2): *"steg beläggningen tydligt — men
främst på vardagar, och bara på de längsta delsträckorna."* Directional (price cut → occupancy),
hedged (*främst/bara*), scoped (weekdays, longest legs).
- **A = overgeneralisation** — swaps the two explicit scope-limits for *"Varje … oavsett … oavsett"*.
- **B = reversed_causality** — genuine subject/object flip: high occupancy → state cuts price
  (the arrow reversed), vs the text's price → occupancy.
- **D = scope_shift** — lifts the per-trip cost (p3) as if it answered "what happened to occupancy".
- Stem narrows to *beläggningen* so the p3 cost fact cannot be defended as an alternate answer.

**Q2 — `forfattarens_hallning`** (key **A**). Stance the author explicitly asserts: judge night
rail as infrastructure, same yardstick as road and flight (p1 + p4).
- **B = stance_inversion** — the author's valence flipped to "loss item, should be phased out";
  labelled *stance_inversion*, **not** reversed_causality (no causal/subject-object arrow is
  reversed — this is a stance/valence flip, per the batch3 labelling rule).
- **C = detail_as_main** — promotes the *council's* caution (*ett enda vinterförsök*) to the
  author's stance and OVER-hedges (*möjligen*, *för tidigt att uttala sig*); the author explicitly
  pushes past it (*"fel slutsats att de därför saknar värde"*).
- **D = plausible_worldknowledge** — a flight ban sounds like a plausible climate stance but the
  author wants equal treatment, never a ban.

## Hedge/length-tell breaking
Q1 key is the hedged option (the classic overgen/reversed frame). **Q2 inverts the correlation**:
the key (A) is the confident, unhedged stance and distractor C is the OVER-hedged option — so
"pick the qualified answer" fails on Q2. Key letters spread **C, A**. M-TELL confirms the key is
the longest option in **0/2** questions (Q1 longest=B, Q2 longest=C).

## Self-blind-solve (skeptical, passage-only)
- **Q1 → C.** A dies on the two explicit scope caveats; B is the reversed arrow; D answers a
  different paragraph. Single defensible answer.
- **Q2 → A.** B is the stance the author calls *bakvänd*; C mistakes the cited council's caution
  for the author's (confident) stance; D imports an unheld ban. Single defensible answer.
Neither item is two-way.
