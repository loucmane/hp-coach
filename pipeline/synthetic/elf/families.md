# ELF item families — taxonomy, frequency, trap anatomy

Human-readable companion to `families.json` (generated by
`scripts/build_families.py`). The families join three evidence layers:

1. **Layer 1** (`frameworks/elf_taxonomy.json`): 8 cognitive-op types
   (ELF-TYPE-001…008), authored 2026-05-11 from a manual pass over all 540
   ELF items, with curated attack protocols and distractor patterns.
2. **Layer 2** (`data/explanations/*.json`): every ELF item carries a
   `framework_id` tag plus per-distractor `why_tempting` / `why_wrong` prose —
   the trap-anatomy source.
3. **Format map** (`scripts/out/corpus_stats.json`): which block format each
   qid lives in.

**Cloze is a family here even though Layer 1 has no type for it**: the 135
gapped-text items (25% of all ELF) carry `framework_id: null` in Layer 2
because they are a *format*, not a reading-comprehension cognitive op. For
generation they need their own contract, so they are `ELF-CLOZE-001`.

Difficulty spread: **unknown for all families.** Item-level Elo ratings live
in the production D1 database and are not exported to the repo; UHR publishes
only sitting-level normering bands (`data/normering/`). Layer 1's notes give
qualitative rankings only (e.g. TYPE-001 easiest when read carefully;
TYPE-002/005 are the persistent error pockets under dense prose).

Structural readings of this table:

- **Detail retrieval is the backbone** (29%) and is format-agnostic.
- **Main idea skews toward short texts** (26 of 53 in bundles) — the 1q short
  text is *usually* either a detail probe or a gist probe.
- **Tone/attitude and purpose live almost exclusively in long passages**
  (21/31 and 12/15) — you need extended voice to have a stance.
- **A generated long-passage 5q block** should therefore budget approximately:
  2× TYPE-001, 1× TYPE-002/003, 1× TYPE-004 (at position 1 or 5 — the
  main-idea item is edge-positioned in 16/20 observed cases), 1× TYPE-005/006/007.

## Trap anatomy per family

Two evidence grades. **Curated** = Layer 1 `common_distractors` (authored,
reliable). **Mined** = regex-tagged over Layer 2 `why_tempting`/`why_wrong`
prose (coarse — roughly half of distractor rationales stay untagged, so mined
counts are floors, not shares of truth; see `build_families.py` TRAP_TAGS).

### ELF-TYPE-001 Direct detail retrieval (159 items)
Curated: true-statement-from-wrong-paragraph; plausible outside knowledge not
in the text; quantifier upgrade (some→all, often→always); causal link glued
onto a descriptive statement. Mined confirms: quantifier_upgrade dominates
(72 tagged distractors), with tone_misread and attribution swaps as minor
modes. **Generator rule: every TYPE-001 distractor must be one of these four
transformations of real passage sentences — never random falsehoods.**

### ELF-CLOZE-001 Gapped text (135 items)
Mined: collocation_misfit is the signature trap (69) — the wrong word has the
right *shape* (same POS, same suffix, vaguely thematic: "durability" for
"austerity") but no English collocation with the frame ("years of ___").
Second mode: polarity — a contrastive "but/yet" upstream demands the
negative-pole option; skimmers pick the positive one ("prosperity"). Third:
register/tone fit and connective logic (sentence-initial adverb gaps:
Consequently vs Historically). **Generator rule: each gap's 4 options are
POS-uniform, 1–2 words, ≥2 of them suffix- or shape-matched to the key; one
distractor must be the polarity mirror when the frame contains a contrast.**

### ELF-TYPE-002 Inference / implication (63 items)
Curated: literal restatement (too close — retrieval, not inference);
two-step leaps requiring outside knowledge; quantifier upgrades. The key
sits "one logical inch beyond the text, never two" (Layer 1 protocol).

### ELF-TYPE-004 Main idea (53 items)
Curated traps: a vivid sub-point promoted to "main"; a title-plausible theme
the text never argues; scope too broad. Edge-positioned in the 5q block.

### ELF-TYPE-005 Stance / tone (31 items)
Mined signature: **role_or_attribution_swap leads (18)** — the distractor
assigns a quoted critic's stance to the writer, or vice versa. Passages with
quotes are the natural habitat. Second mode: polarity of hedged evaluations
(mildly skeptical ≠ dismissive).

### ELF-TYPE-003 / 006 / 007 / 008
Lower-frequency ops; Layer 1 carries their protocols. TYPE-007 ("which
statement is most in line with the text") inverts TYPE-001: four candidate
claims audited against the whole text. TYPE-008 has only 7 items — real but
marginal; do not over-generate it (Layer 1 flags it "on the floor").

### ELF-UNTAGGED (22 items)
Layer-2 explanations without a framework tag, scattered across both passes and
all three reading formats. Spot-checked prompts (host-2014-verb2-ELF-037,
host-2016-verb1-ELF-032, host-2021-verb1-ELF-032, host-2021-verb2-ELF-040):
ordinary detail/agreement/inference items with slightly unusual stem wording
("What fits best with…", "What is assumed to be…") that the tagging pass
skipped. Not a hidden tenth family; treat as tagging noise, do not model.
